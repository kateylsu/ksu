Pipeline Products comparison: old (without selfcal) vs. new (with selfcal)

generated date is: 2024-10-24

JWST pipeline 1.15 has a new selfcal function in spec2 stage to reduce the negative spikes in the data. Here is the documentation, along with the notebook on how to run. Followings are a comparison using HD 23514 MRS data.

Each Ch/Bd has a table showing three plots (Ch4 data are not shown too noisy.)

Top row: direct comparison (red solid is old (w/o selfcal), blue dash is new (w/ selfcal), and the green is the new one but offset for viewing clarify

Middle row: my auto continuum subtraction (without custom flagging data) with median S/N given on the plot. Red color are the data pionts used to derive the fitted continuum shown as the green dash.

Bottom row: comparison with CY's model and old reduced spectrum. Orange line is CY's continuum-subtracted spectrum that was used for line modeling, while black is mine using the selfcal product. Dark and light gray areas show +/- 1- and 3-sigma boundaries. Color regions show CY's model line emission: purple for CO, blue for H2O, red for CO2, while NH3 model is shown as the green line (not region).

Here are my conclusions:

  1. Selfcal does improve to reduce the reltaively 'negative' points in the data, but not always. In fact, it can 'create' new ones that are not seen in the old data (I wonder why?)
  2. In the bottom row (data compared with model), orange (w/o selfcal) and black (w/ selfcal) agree most of the time, but I do think black is a bit better in the number of negative points, but they are very similar for bright (well detected >3 sigma points). I think the model does a good job reproducing most of the data that are detected above 3-sigma (i.e., CO2 lines).
  3. I am not sure whether it is worthy to retry the fit for the paper?
