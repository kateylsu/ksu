Personal Information

Research Interests

  • Debris Disk Evolution: investigating the thermal dust emission around stars at different ages (from main-sequence stars to white dwarfs) to further understand the origin, evolution and fate of our Solar System.
  • Debris Disk Modeling: modeling the distributions and properties of dust in circumstellar disks using spatially resolved imaging data and/or global spectral energy distributions.
  • Planetary Systems: Investigating the implication of debris disks on planet formation and evolution and understanding the observational characteristics of debris disks as tracers of planet formation
  • Dust around Evolved Stars: investigating the dust distribution around Planetary Nebulae, Proto-Planetary Nebulae, and Nova-like variables using Spitzer Space Telescope in the thermal infrared and Hubble Space Telescope in the visible wavelengths.
